Brazil is the largest country in South America bounded by the Atlantic Ocean. Neighboring countries include Argentina, Bolivia, Colombia, French Guiana, Guyana, Paraguay, Peru, Suriname, Uruguay, and Venezuela.
A geographically large country, the terrain includes lowlands, hills, and some mountains. The Amazon River runs through northeastern Brazil, and the Amazon Rainforest covers about one-third of the country. The government system is a federal republic; the chief of state and head of government is the president. The economic system is a market economy in which the prices of goods and services are determined in a free price system. Brazil is a member of the Common Market of the South (Mercosur).Resource

General facts about Brazil

  1. Name: Federative Republic of Brazil
  2. States: 26
  3. Largest city: São Paulo
  4. Currency: Brazilian Real (BRL or R$). US$1 = approx R$4
  5. National day: September 7th
  6. National anthem: National Anthem of Brazil by Francisco Manuel da Silva
  7. Dialing code: +55
  8. Time zones: Fernando de Noronha (GMT-2), Brasilia (GMT-3), Amazon (GMT-4), and Acre (GMT-5)
  9. Brazilian life expectancy: 75 years
